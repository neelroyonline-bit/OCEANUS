ü§î WHAT IF Scenarios

What if the Titanic had hit the iceberg head-on?

Counterintuitively, she might have survived! A head-on collision would have crushed the bow but kept the watertight compartments intact. By turning, the iceberg scraped along the side, opening 6 compartments ‚Äî she could only float with 4 flooded. The ship was doomed by trying to avoid the berg.

What if we could raise the Titanic?

Impossible with current technology! At 3,800m depth under 6,000 PSI pressure, the wreck is too fragile. The hull is being consumed by "rusticles" (iron-eating bacteria) at 400 lbs/day. Scientists estimate the Titanic will completely collapse by 2030. It's better preserved as an underwater memorial.

What if all undiscovered shipwrecks were found?

UNESCO estimates 3 million shipwrecks remain undiscovered worldwide. If found, they would contain over $60 billion in treasure, rewrite countless historical events, and solve mysteries like the fate of explorer ships, ancient trading routes, and lost civilizations. The ocean floor is the world's largest museum.
